Key takeaways

Stereo isn't just "left and right"‚Äîit's about creating the illusion of horizontal space using differences in timing, phase, and amplitude between speakers.

Your brain uses tiny differences (milliseconds, subtle volume changes) to perceive where sounds are coming from. Stereo processing exploits this.

Most individual elements in your mix are mono (vocals, bass, kick). Width comes from how you place those mono elements in the stereo field.
