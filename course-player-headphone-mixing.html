Why headphones lie (and how to compensate)

Understanding the fundamental differences between headphones and speakers, and what it means for your mixes.

Key takeaways

Headphones place sounds directly in each ear with no crossfeed, making stereo width seem wider than it actually is. What sounds wide on headphones often collapses on speakers.

Headphones emphasize bass and low-mids differently than speakers in a room. Your headphone mix will likely have too much low end if you don't compensate.

The sweet spot: Mix on headphones, but check key decisions on speakers or with reference tracks. Don't trust either source alone.
