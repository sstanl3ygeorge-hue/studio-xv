Understanding Low End

The physics and perception of low frequencies, and why they behave differently than mids and highs.

In this lesson

  • ‚Üí How low frequencies work in a mix
  • ‚Üí Why bass is harder to control
  • ‚Üí The frequency ranges that matter
  • ‚Üí Listening environment considerations

Key takeaways

Low frequencies contain more energy and take up more space in a mix than any other frequency range.

What sounds good on headphones often doesn't translate to speakers because low end behaves differently in different spaces.

The sub-bass range (20-60Hz) is felt more than heard. The bass range (60-250Hz) is where most problems occur.
