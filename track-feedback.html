Send a Track for Feedback

Asynchronous mix and production feedback for when you don't need a full session—just honest, actionable notes on what's working and what needs adjustment.

How It Works

Send your track (mix, master, or work-in-progress). I'll listen on multiple systems, take notes, and send back detailed written feedback covering what's working, what's not, and specific suggestions for improvement.

Turnaround is typically 48–72 hours. You'll receive a document breaking down technical issues, creative observations, and prioritized next steps. This isn't a mixing service—I won't fix your track for you. But you'll know exactly what needs attention and how to approach it.

What Feedback Includes

  • Mix balance: Level relationships, frequency balance, stereo image
  • Technical issues: Clipping, phase problems, masking, resonances
  • Arrangement notes: Pacing, structure, transitions, energy flow
  • Production observations: Sound selection, layering, processing
  • Reference comparison: How your track sits next to professional releases
  • Priority list: What to fix first for the biggest improvement

What This Is Best For

Track feedback works well when you're close to finishing a mix but something feels off. Or when you want an outside perspective before committing to a master. Or when you need to know if your track is release-ready.

It's also useful for producers who prefer written notes they can reference while working, rather than live sessions. Some people learn better by reading and implementing at their own pace.

Good Candidates:

  • Tracks that are 80–90% finished but need objective feedback
  • Mixes that sound good in your room but don't translate elsewhere
  • Projects where you're unsure if they're ready for mastering
  • Tracks you want compared against reference material

What This Isn't

This is not mixing or mastering. I won't process your files or send back a revised version. If you need hands-on work, that's a different service.

This also isn't a substitute for learning. If you're early in your production journey and need foundational guidance, one-to-one lessons will be more useful. Feedback assumes you already know how to implement suggestions—you just need direction on what to implement.

File Requirements

Send your track as a WAV or high-quality MP3. Include any reference tracks you're comparing against, along with notes about what you're trying to achieve or what you're unsure about.

If you're sending stems for more detailed feedback, zip the folder and use a file transfer service (WeTransfer, Dropbox, Google Drive, etc.). Make sure files are properly labeled and organized.

Pricing

Bulk packages available for EPs and albums. Contact for details.

How to Submit

Email your track to feedback@studioxv.co.uk with:

  • Your track (attached or link)
  • Any reference tracks (optional)
  • Notes about what you're working toward or what concerns you have
  • Your preferred payment method (invoice will be sent before work begins)

Not Sure What You Need?

If you're torn between written feedback and a live lesson, here's how to decide:

  • Choose feedback if your track is nearly done and you just need an objective assessment
  • Choose a lesson if you need to learn techniques, solve workflow issues, or want real-time guidance

Still unsure? Send an email describing where you're at, and I'll recommend the best approach.
