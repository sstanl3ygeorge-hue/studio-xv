What is sidechaining?

Understanding the fundamental concept of sidechain compression and how it works.

In this lesson

  • ‚Üí The basic principle of sidechaining
  • ‚Üí How sidechain compression differs from standard compression
  • ‚Üí Real-world examples of ducking and pumping
  • ‚Üí When to use sidechain in your mixes

Key takeaways

Sidechain compression uses one signal to control the compression of another, creating space and movement in your mix.

Unlike standard compression that responds to the compressed signal itself, sidechaining responds to an external trigger signal.

The two main applications are ducking (making space) and pumping (creating rhythm), and understanding which you need is the first step.
