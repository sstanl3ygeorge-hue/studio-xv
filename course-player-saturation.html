What saturation actually does

Understanding saturation beyond "making things sound warmer" and why it matters in modern production.

In this lesson

Key takeaways

Saturation is controlled distortion. It adds harmonics‚Äîadditional frequencies that weren't in the original signal‚Äîwhich makes sounds feel richer, fuller, and more present.

Unlike EQ, which boosts existing frequencies, saturation creates new ones. This is why saturated sounds cut through a mix better than simply turning them up.

Digital saturation models analog hardware behavior, but it's not about "warm vs. cold"‚Äîit's about adding musical complexity to otherwise sterile digital signals.
